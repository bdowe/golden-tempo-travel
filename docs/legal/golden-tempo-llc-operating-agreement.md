# Operating Agreement of Golden Tempo LLC

**A New Jersey Limited Liability Company**

> **DRAFT — review before signing.** This draft is written for a single-member
> New Jersey LLC under the New Jersey Revised Uniform Limited Liability Company
> Act (N.J.S.A. 42:2C-1 et seq., "the Act"). It is deliberately structured for
> flexibility: broad purpose, future members, and tax-classification changes
> all work without rewriting it. Have an attorney review it if/when the stakes
> rise (outside investment, Phase 2 merchant sales, or a second member).

This Operating Agreement (the "Agreement") is made effective as of
__________________, 20____ (the "Effective Date") by **Brian Dowe** (the
"Member"), the sole member of **Golden Tempo LLC** (the "Company").

## Article I — Formation

1.1 **Formation.** The Company was formed as a New Jersey limited liability
company by the filing of a Certificate of Formation with the New Jersey
Division of Revenue and Enterprise Services on __________________, 20____.

1.2 **Name.** The business of the Company shall be conducted under the name
"Golden Tempo LLC" or such alternate names (including registered DBAs such as
"Golden Tempo Travel") as the Member may adopt.

1.3 **Purpose.** The Company is organized to engage in **any lawful business
activity** for which a limited liability company may be organized under the
Act, including without limitation the development and operation of software
products and travel-related services. No amendment to this Agreement is
required for the Company to enter new lines of business.

1.4 **Registered Agent and Office.** The Company shall continuously maintain a
registered agent and registered office in New Jersey as required by the Act.
The Member may change the registered agent or office at any time by filing
with the State.

1.5 **Principal Office.** The principal office of the Company shall be at such
location (within or outside New Jersey) as the Member may determine. The
Company may conduct business remotely and from changing locations.

1.6 **Term.** The Company's existence is perpetual unless dissolved under
Article VIII.

## Article II — Member; Membership Interests

2.1 **Sole Member.** The Member owns one hundred percent (100%) of the
membership interests in the Company, denominated as **1,000,000 Units**.
Units exist for administrative convenience and future flexibility; they do not
create additional classes of interest unless and until created under §2.3.

2.2 **Capital Contribution.** The Member has contributed, or will contribute,
initial capital as recorded in the Company's records. The Member is not
obligated to make additional contributions.

2.3 **Admission of Additional Members; New Classes.** The Member may, without
amending this Agreement, (a) admit one or more additional members, (b) issue
additional Units or create new classes or series of Units (including
non-voting or profit-only interests), and (c) adopt an amended and restated
operating agreement reflecting a multi-member structure. Upon admission of a
second member, the Member shall cause this Agreement to be amended and
restated as a multi-member agreement, and until then this Agreement continues
to govern.

2.4 **Transferability.** The Member may transfer all or part of the Member's
Units at any time. A transferee becomes a member upon the transfer, this being
the consent required by the Act.

## Article III — Management

3.1 **Member-Managed.** The Company is **member-managed**. The Member has full
and exclusive authority to manage the Company's business and affairs and to
bind the Company.

3.2 **Authority.** Without limitation, the Member may open and operate bank
and payment accounts, enter contracts (including affiliate, partner, and API
agreements), engage employees and contractors, obtain insurance, borrow money,
and settle claims, all in the name of the Company.

3.3 **Officers.** The Member may appoint officers or agents with delegated
authority, and may revoke any delegation at any time.

3.4 **No Personal Liability.** Pursuant to the Act, the debts, obligations,
and liabilities of the Company are solely those of the Company. The Member is
not personally liable for them by reason of being a member or acting as a
manager.

## Article IV — Separateness

The Member shall cause the Company to: (a) maintain one or more bank accounts
in the Company's name used exclusively for Company business; (b) keep Company
funds separate from personal funds and not commingle them; (c) sign Company
contracts in the Company's name; (d) maintain books and records adequate to
show the Company's finances separately; and (e) keep the Company adequately
capitalized for its foreseeable obligations.

## Article V — Tax Matters

5.1 **Default Classification.** While the Company has a single member it is
disregarded as an entity separate from its owner for U.S. federal income tax
purposes, and its activity is reported on the Member's individual returns.

5.2 **Change of Classification.** The Member may at any time elect a different
federal tax classification for the Company (including an election to be taxed
as an S corporation or C corporation) by filing the applicable IRS forms. No
amendment to this Agreement is required for such an election.

5.3 **Tax Year; Records.** The Company's tax year is the calendar year. The
Company shall retain financial records for at least seven (7) years.

## Article VI — Distributions

The Member may cause the Company to distribute cash or property to the Member
at any time, provided the distribution is not prohibited by the Act (including
the solvency limitations of N.J.S.A. 42:2C-34).

## Article VII — Indemnification

To the fullest extent permitted by the Act, the Company shall indemnify and
hold harmless the Member (and any officer or agent appointed under §3.3) for
losses, claims, and expenses incurred by reason of acting on behalf of the
Company, except to the extent arising from that person's own willful
misconduct or knowing violation of law. The Company may purchase insurance for
this purpose.

## Article VIII — Dissolution

The Company dissolves only upon (a) the Member's written election to dissolve,
or (b) entry of a decree of judicial dissolution under the Act. Upon
dissolution, the Company's affairs shall be wound up, assets applied first to
creditors, and the remainder distributed to the Member.

## Article IX — Death or Incapacity of the Member; Successor

9.1 **Successor.** Upon the death or permanent incapacity of the Member, the
Member's personal representative, or the transferee designated in the Member's
will or revocable trust, shall automatically be admitted as a substitute
member with all rights of the Member, without further consent. This section is
intended to prevent dissolution under N.J.S.A. 42:2C-48(a)(3) by ensuring a
member exists at all times.

9.2 The Company shall continue and not dissolve by reason of the Member's
death or incapacity.

## Article X — Miscellaneous

10.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of
New Jersey. If the Company is later **domesticated into another jurisdiction**
under N.J.S.A. 42:2C-83 et seq. (or successor provisions), this Agreement
remains in effect and shall thereafter be governed by the laws of the new
jurisdiction of formation, without interruption of the Company's existence.

10.2 **Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only in a writing signed by
the Member.

10.3 **Entire Agreement; Severability.** This Agreement is the entire
operating agreement of the Company. If any provision is unenforceable, the
remainder stays in effect.

10.4 **Default Rules.** Where this Agreement is silent, the default rules of
the Act govern.
